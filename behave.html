Shark Behaviors

How do sharks communicate?

Shark use their bodies to communicate. For example, a shark may arch their body and open their mouth to show that they feel threatened. Other animals like snakes and hippos uses this technique.

Sharks combine physical adaptations such as sharp teeth, heightened senses and a forceful body and tail with behavioral techniques to catch prey. Sharks are nocturnal predators of the ocean, feeding at night between low and high tide, and typically in shallow water near reefs.

Sharks like the whale sharks sharks with small teeth mostly prey on small fish and tend to be more passive. While sharks with big teeth like the great white prey on bigger fishes and even other sharks and tend to be aggressive.

Shark attacks on humans most often than not is a result of their bad eyesight. They mistake us for seals.
